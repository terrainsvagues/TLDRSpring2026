## Geographical Hotspots—Discrepancies in Book Banning Across States

<sup>Yasmine, Avery, Andrew, Allison</sup>

### Regional Patterns in Book Banning

This project has exposed several regions with an abnormally large number of book bans. Specifically, we can see from our map that Texas and Florida have banned more books than other states, and the Southern United States in general have banned almost as many books as the rest of the nation combined. According to PEN America, in 2022, the states with the largest number of books banned that year were Texas, South Carolina, and Florida, highlighting a clear geographic trend in the regions where book banning is most prevalent ([Holzworth](https://southernreviewofbooks.com/2023/08/23/banned-books-in-the-south/)). For example, one of the books banned in Florida is [*And Tango Makes Three*](https://terrainsvagues.github.io/TLDRSpring2026/items/07group1.html), a children's book that depicts male homosexual penguins raising a third penguin. One of the main reasons the book was banned was for its depiction of homosexual material, even though the book's main themes revolve around acceptance, family, and nature. In a conservative state like Florida, topics such as gay rights may be seen as too radical, even in children's books such as *And Tango Makes Three*. This is shown through our class's metadata, where nearly 20% of books in Florida were banned due to LGBTQ or sexual material.

### Conservative States and Historical Trends

Historically, we've seen that more conservative states tend to ban books at a higher rate. The South has history with slavery and deep religious ties, and that history seeps into their choices today. Connecting back to our theme of geography correlating with book banning rates, the South also has a higher concentration of conservative states than any other national region. Florida, Iowa, and Texas, which are all considered conservative, recorded the most book bans in 2023 and 2024 ([PEN America](https://pen.org/report/beyond-the-shelves/)), with Iowa and Florida specifically banning more books than all other states combined. Generally, the banned books tend to go against conservative ideas, and as such they tend to face backlash from local communities, leading to them being banned.

### The Current Landscape

Today, there are many groups wanting to control what is read. Christian nationalists have introduced policies to protect children from ideas that are considered harmful to their development, and many parents are limiting the content their children are exposed to outside of the household. For example, in 2024, Florida governor Ron DeSantis signed a bill into law that made banning books easier, as the previous 2022 law was too broad ([Southern Poverty Law Center](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/stories/book-challenges-laws-deep-south/)). Book bannings have also worsened with Trump's policies against DEI. Book bans serve as another tool to further restrict ideas in certain regions, which can be dangerous for the ability of a population to think and act independently of a system. This is similar to historical book bans from other countries around the world, continuing a trend of book bans being used by individuals in power to limit the abilities of their populations to remain in power.
